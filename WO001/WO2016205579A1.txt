abstract
    A method of carrying out combustion of fuel with air/O2 in a furnace (such as a glass making furnace) is disclosed, where at least a part of the combustion products (e.g. H20, CO2) are passed with hydrocarbon fuel to a regenerator where an endothermic reaction forms syngas (H2 and CO) and generates soot (C-particulates), which is then passed to and combusted in the furnace with an oxidant (air/O2) to improve the performance w.r.t. waste heat recovery efficiency. The method can be applied to an end-(preferably O2-)fired GMF with reversible regenerators, using CH4 as the injected hydrocarbon fuel.
